CHRIST THE REDEEMER


Vincentian priest Pedro Maria Boss first suggested placing a Christian monument
on Mount Corcovado in the mid-1850s to honor Princess Isabel, regent of Brazil and the daughter of Emperor Pedro II, but the project was not approved.[1] In 1889, the country became a republic,
and owing to the separation of church and state, the proposed statue was dismissed.
